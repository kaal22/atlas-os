## Weather vs climate
- **Weather** is what it is like outside today (rain, wind, temperature).
- **Climate** is the usual pattern of weather over many years.

## Measuring weather
- Temperature (°C), rainfall (mm), wind speed and direction, cloud cover.
- Keep a one-week weather diary: morning and afternoon observations.

## Seasons
Many places have spring, summer, autumn (fall), and winter because Earth tilts as it
orbits the Sun. Near the equator, seasons may be wet and dry instead.

## Water and land
Mountains can make one side wetter (rain shadow). Coasts are often milder than inland
areas. Deserts are dry; rainforests are warm and wet.

## Looking after our place
Reduce waste, reuse bags, recycle when you can, and care for local green spaces.
Small habits add up.

## Challenge
Make a simple rain gauge from a clear jar with marks. Measure rainfall for a week and
draw a bar chart.
