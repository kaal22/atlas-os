# Maths basics (ages 6–8)

## Counting and place value
- Count forwards and backwards to 100.
- Tens and ones: 47 is 4 tens and 7 ones.
- Compare numbers using greater than, less than, and equal.
- Odd and even: 2, 4, 6 are even; 1, 3, 5 are odd.

## Addition and subtraction
- Number bonds to 10 and 20 (e.g. 7 + 3 = 10, 12 + 8 = 20).
- Use a number line to jump forward when adding and back when subtracting.
- Check answers by doing the inverse operation.
- Add three single-digit numbers by finding a friendly pair first (e.g. 6 + 4 + 3).

## Early multiplication
- Skip-count in 2s, 5s, and 10s.
- Arrays: 3 rows of 4 counters make 12.
- Double and half: double 6 is 12; half of 10 is 5.

## Money and measure
- Recognise coins and notes used in your country.
- Measure length with a ruler in centimetres.
- Tell time to the hour and half-hour on an analogue clock.

## Practice questions
1. What is 8 + 5?
2. What is 14 − 6?
3. How many tens are in 63?
4. What is double 9?
5. Count in 5s: 5, 10, 15, ___, ___

## Answers
1. 13  
2. 8  
3. 6 tens  
4. 18  
5. 20, 25
