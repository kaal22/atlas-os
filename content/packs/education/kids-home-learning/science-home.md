## Water cycle (short)
Water evaporates from puddles and seas, forms clouds, then falls as rain or snow.
Try: leave a shallow dish of water in a sunny window and mark the level each day.

## States of matter
- Solid: keeps its shape (ice cube).
- Liquid: takes the shape of its container (water).
- Gas: fills the space available (steam / water vapour).
Heating can melt or evaporate; cooling can freeze or condense.

## Magnets
Magnets attract iron and steel. Opposite poles attract; like poles push apart.
Try: sort fridge magnets, coins, plastic, and wood by what sticks.

## Forces
A force is a push or a pull. Friction slows things down. Gravity pulls objects toward Earth.
Try: roll a toy car on carpet vs smooth floor — which goes farther? Why?

## Plants need
Light, water, air, and soil (or nutrients). Keep a bean in a jar with wet paper towel
and watch roots grow over a week.

## Safety
Never taste experiments. Wash hands after outdoor collecting. Ask an adult before using
heat, sharp tools, or household cleaners.
