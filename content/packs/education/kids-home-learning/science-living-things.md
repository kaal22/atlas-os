# Living things (ages 7–10)

Atlas home-learning starter. Licence: CC BY 4.0.

## What is alive?
Living things typically move (or grow toward light), sense their surroundings, grow,
reproduce, need nutrition, and respire (use oxygen / release energy from food).

## Habitats
A habitat is where a living thing finds food, water, shelter, and space.
Examples: pond, woodland, seashore, grassland, city park.

## Food chains
Sun → plant (producer) → plant-eater (herbivore) → animal-eater (carnivore).
If one link is removed, others can be affected.

## Human body basics
- Bones support the body; muscles move it.
- The heart pumps blood; lungs take in oxygen.
- Teeth: incisors cut, canines tear, molars grind — brush twice a day.

## Classification game
Sort household items or outdoor finds into: living, once-living, never-living.
Discuss why a wooden chair is “once-living” but a rock is not.

## Observation challenge
Choose one plant or insect near home. Record for five days: weather, what it looks
like, and one change you notice.
