# Internet

The Internet is a global system of interconnected computer networks that use the Internet
protocol suite (TCP/IP) to communicate.

## Origins
Research networks such as ARPANET (1960s–70s) led to packet-switched networking. The modern
Internet grew through academic, commercial, and public adoption from the 1980s onward.
The World Wide Web (early 1990s) made hyperlinked documents widely accessible via browsers.

## Building blocks
- **IP addresses** identify hosts; **DNS** maps names to addresses.
- **Routers** forward packets between networks.
- Application protocols include HTTP/HTTPS, SMTP, and SSH.

## Society
The Internet supports communication, commerce, education, and media, while raising issues of
privacy, security, accessibility, and governance.
