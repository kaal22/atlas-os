# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria convert
light energy into chemical energy stored in sugars.

## Overview
In plants, chlorophyll in chloroplasts captures sunlight. Carbon dioxide from air and
water from roots are combined to produce glucose and oxygen:
`6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂` (simplified).

## Why it matters
Photosynthesis is the foundation of most food chains and releases the oxygen animals breathe.
Factors that affect the rate include light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration, and temperature.

## Related ideas
Cellular respiration releases energy from sugars; it is roughly the reverse of photosynthesis
in overall inputs and outputs.
