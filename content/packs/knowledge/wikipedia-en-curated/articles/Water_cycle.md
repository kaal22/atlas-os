# Water cycle

The water cycle (hydrologic cycle) describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and
below Earth’s surface.

## Main processes
- **Evaporation** and **transpiration** move water into the atmosphere.
- **Condensation** forms clouds.
- **Precipitation** returns water as rain, snow, or hail.
- **Infiltration** and **runoff** move water into soil, rivers, lakes, and oceans.

## Importance
The cycle redistributes fresh water, shapes weather and landscapes, and supports ecosystems.
Human activities (dams, groundwater pumping, deforestation) can alter local parts of the cycle.
