Climate change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. Since the

mid-20th century, human activities — especially burning fossil fuels — have been the main

driver of observed global warming through greenhouse gas emissions.

Greenhouse effect

Gases such as carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O) trap heat in

Earth’s atmosphere. Higher concentrations raise average global temperatures.

Impacts

Effects include rising sea levels, more intense heatwaves, changing rainfall, ice melt, and

stresses on ecosystems, food systems, and coastal communities.

Responses

Mitigation reduces emissions (clean energy, efficiency, land use). Adaptation prepares for

impacts already underway (flood defences, resilient crops, early warning systems).
