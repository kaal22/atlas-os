Gravity

Gravity is the fundamental interaction that attracts bodies with mass toward one another.

On Earth, it gives weight to physical objects and causes objects to fall when dropped.

Newton and Einstein

Isaac Newton described gravity as a force between masses (inverse-square law). Albert Einstein’s

general relativity describes gravity as curvature of spacetime caused by mass–energy; it

explains phenomena such as the bending of light near the Sun and precise planetary orbits.

Everyday and space

Gravitational acceleration near Earth’s surface is about 9.8 m/s². Gravity keeps the Moon in

orbit and holds the Solar System and galaxies together on large scales.
